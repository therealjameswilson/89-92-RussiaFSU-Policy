    Compiler Precedents

    Learn From The Reagan Soviet Volumes

    Open precedent report

    Same Editorial Lineage

    Elizabeth C. Charles edited the prior Reagan Soviet Volumes IV and V. Together they model a dense policy chronology: 692 main-series documents plus appendix material, arranged around turning points rather than file provenance.

    Chapter Logic

    The precedent chapters move by crisis, negotiation cycle, leadership change, and summit preparation. Volume IV should use the same rhythm for Malta, German unification spillover, the August coup, recognition, nuclear inheritance, assistance, Nunn-Lugar, and START II.

    Source Mix

    The Reagan volumes combine White House/NSC files, State Executive Secretariat lots, country and head-of-state files, staff files, cable traffic, intelligence, Defense, published records, and editorial notes. That argues for preserving source lanes beside the dated document list.

    First Footnotes

    The source notes carry repository, subfile, document state, classification, drafting history, transmission context, and cross-references. This page should treat that provenance as selection metadata, not cleanup at the end.
